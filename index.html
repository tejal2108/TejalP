Identification of Urban & Rural | GitHub

This project applies machine learning to classify images into rural or urban categories using remote sensing and computer vision. The system is trained on a labeled dataset of satellite or ground-level images to distinguish between the two environments.

The BRISK (Binary Robust Invariant Scalable Keypoints) algorithm is used for feature extraction, and classifiers like K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Support Vector Machines (SVM), Decision Trees (DT), Random Forests (RF), and Logistic Regression (LR) are evaluated.

KNN achieved the highest accuracy of 98% with K=3 clusters. This model has practical applications in urban planning, disaster relief, environmental monitoring, and resource allocation.

Smart Accidental Alert and Location Tracking System Using Telegram Bot and GPS Technology | Link

This project addresses the critical issue of road accidents, especially in regions with limited traffic signals or infrastructure. The goal is to detect accidents in real-time and send immediate alerts, including the accident’s location, to emergency contacts or hospitals using a Telegram bot and GPS technology. The system relies on an accelerometer for accident detection and a GPS module for location tracking, ensuring that help can be dispatched promptly to reduce fatalities.

While the system offers high accuracy in detecting accidents and sharing the location, its performance is dependent on reliable internet connectivity. This solution has great potential for enhancing the timeliness of medical assistance, particularly in areas with high accident rates and limited rescue services.
